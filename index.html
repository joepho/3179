Corruption Across the Globe

An analysis of various metrics used to perceive corruption across the world.

Joseph Phoparesut

CPI Rankings

The Corruption Perception Index (CPI) is a measurement index that evaluates the level of perceived corruption across the world. Based on a number of various metrics which are evaluated largely from surveys, a CPI score is assigned to each country. From that score a rank of the least to most corrupt countries in the world.

Global CPI Scores

One of the most immediately obvious patterns within the CPI is that scores show very little regional variation - countries located in the same geographic region tend to have very similar CPI scores. (higher scores indicate less perceived corruption).

It may also be noted that CPI scores tend to show little fluctuation over smaller periods of time. This is largely because corruption perception is measured on the opinions of a country's people, which cannot be culturally altered in short time spans.

Metric 1 - Reporting Likelihood

One of the metrics that may influence the CPI of a country is how likely its residents are to report corruption as they see it. Intuitively, it would seem that countries with residents that are more likely to report corruption would have more accounts of corruption, and therefore a lower CPI score.

Likelihood to Report Corruption by Region

Here, we examine the likelihood of a certain country's residents to report corruption against its CPI (2020) score to determine any correlation between the two metrics.

Contrary to the intuitive hypothesis, it would appear that the likelihood of report has little correlation with the CPI of a given country.

This is apparent with MENA possessing a higher likelihood and a low CPI score (which is intuitively in line), contrasting against ECA with almost half the likelihood, but a lower CPI score.

Metric 2 - Bribery

Another metric considered when calculating a CPI score for any given country is the prevalence of bribery.

The following scatter plot allows us to visualise the correlation between bribery rates and CPI score. The data shows that generally countries with lower rates of bribery possess higher CPI scores in comparison to those with lower CPI scores.

It can be concluded from this data that the prevalence of bribery within a country definitively has an influence on its CPI rating, however the presence of existing outliers indicate the influence of the other parameters used to calculate the CPI.
